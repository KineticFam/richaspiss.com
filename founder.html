Robert Bromford Ward II

Founder & Creative Director

Robert Bromford Ward II didn't grow up in luxury. He grew up around it — close enough to study it, far enough to see through it. While other brands whisper, Ward speaks clearly. While they understate, he punctuates.

Rich as Piss is the product of a lifetime spent observing what wealth actually looks like behind closed doors: the irreverence, the humor, the refusal to take oneself too seriously while taking one's craft with absolute seriousness.

"I wanted to build something that felt like the conversation rich people actually have. Not the curated, polished, boring version. The real one. The one where someone pours you a glass of something expensive and says something that makes you laugh until you cry."

Every product in the collection reflects this philosophy. Premium materials. Impeccable construction. A name that stops you in your tracks.

Because anyone can make luxury goods. It takes something else entirely to make luxury goods that make you smile.

Design Philosophy

Ward oversees every detail — from material selection to packaging to the weight of each product in your hand. He believes luxury should engage all the senses, including your sense of humor.

"If you're spending $275 on a paperweight, it should be the most beautiful paperweight you've ever seen. And it should have a name that makes your accountant do a double-take."

The "RAP" crest — a crown over crossed keys, flanked by lions — represents access and audacity. The keys open doors. The crown is self-explanatory. The lions are just showing off.
